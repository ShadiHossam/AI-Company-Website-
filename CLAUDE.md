# AI Company Project — Claude Instructions

## Content Writing Rules

### No em dashes unless grammatically justified
Do not use the em dash (—) in any written content unless it is genuinely the correct punctuation mark for that sentence structure. Overusing em dashes is a strong AI writing signal. When in doubt, rewrite the sentence to use a comma, period, colon, or parentheses instead.

**Avoid:** "We build tools — tools that help teams move faster."
**Better:** "We build tools that help teams move faster."

**Avoid:** "Our platform is simple — and powerful."
**Better:** "Our platform is simple and powerful."

### No AI-sounding words or phrases
Avoid words and phrases that are overused by AI-generated content. They make writing feel generic and robotic. Common offenders to avoid:

- **Adjectives:** seamless, robust, cutting-edge, game-changing, next-generation, innovative, revolutionary, transformative, comprehensive, streamlined, powerful, dynamic, intuitive, groundbreaking, state-of-the-art, world-class, best-in-class
- **Verbs/phrases:** leverage, harness, unlock, empower, supercharge, accelerate, elevate, enhance, propel, foster, facilitate, enable, delve into, dive deep, craft, curate
- **Filler openers:** "In today's fast-paced world...", "In the ever-evolving landscape...", "At [Company], we believe...", "Welcome to the future of...", "Are you tired of..."
- **Vague superlatives:** "the most advanced", "truly unique", "unparalleled", "best-of-breed"

Write the way a sharp, direct human would. Use plain language. Say exactly what you mean.
